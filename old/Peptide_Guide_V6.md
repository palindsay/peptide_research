## 1. GLP-1 Receptor Agonists & Weight-Loss Agents

### 1.1 Semaglutide

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | GLP1-S, Ozempic, Wegovy, Rybelsus, NN9535 |
| **Category** | GLP-1 Receptor Agonist / Incretin Mimetic |
| **Mechanism** | Long-acting GLP-1 receptor agonist. Binds GLP-1R on pancreatic beta cells and hypothalamic appetite centers. Stimulates glucose-dependent insulin secretion, suppresses glucagon, slows gastric emptying, and reduces appetite. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semaglutide)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | STEP 1 (2021): −14.9% body weight at 68 wk ([PMID 33567185](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33567185/)). SELECT Cardiovascular: 20% MACE reduction ([PMID 37952131](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37952131/)). |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed:** Significant weight loss 12–17%; improved HbA1c; 24% reduction in kidney disease progression. |
| **Layman Benefits** | Massive weight loss (up to 15-20%), stops "food noise" (constant hunger thoughts), and protects the heart and kidneys. |
| **Layman Risks** | Frequent nausea, constipation, and vomiting. Risk of "Ozempic Face" (fat loss in face) and rare risk of gallbladder issues. |
| **Typical Dosing** | **FDA label:** 0.25 mg weekly × 4 wk → escalate to **2.4 mg maintenance**. |
| **Side Effects** | Nausea (44%), diarrhea (30%), vomiting (24%). Boxed warning: thyroid C-cell tumors. |
| **Human Research** | Phase III complete. FDA-approved for T2DM (2017) and obesity (2021). |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★★ |
| **Legal Status** | FDA-approved (Rx). |
| **Price Range** | Research peptide: $1.05–$175.00/mg. |
| **Key References** | [PMID 33567185](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33567185/) · [PMID 37952131](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37952131/) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥🔥🔥 Very High — Dominant topic on GLP-1 Forum. |

### 1.2 Tirzepatide

| **Layman Risks** | Standard stomach upset; rare risk of a specific type of thyroid tumor (in animals). |
| **Typical Dosing** | **FDA label:** 2.5 mg SC weekly × 4 wk → escalate to **15 mg max**. |
| **Side Effects** | Nausea (24–33%), diarrhea (17–23%), vomiting (6–12%). |
| **Human Research** | Phase III complete. FDA-approved: T2DM (2022), obesity (2023). |

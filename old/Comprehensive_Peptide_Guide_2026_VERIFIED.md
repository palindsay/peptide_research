| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| ★★★★★ **Very High** | FDA-approved with extensive long-term safety data; endogenous/food-derived |
| ★★★★ **High** | FDA-approved or approved in multiple countries; well-characterized risk profile |

## 1. GLP-1 Agonists & Weight-Loss Peptides

These compounds target incretin pathways (GLP-1, GIP, glucagon, amylin receptors) to suppress appetite, promote satiety, and improve metabolic health.

---

### Semaglutide

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Also Known As** | GLP1-S · Ozempic · Wegovy · Rybelsus |
| **Category** | GLP-1 Receptor Agonist |
| **Mechanism** | Long-acting GLP-1 analogue (94% homology to native GLP-1). Lys26 acylated with C18 diacid for albumin binding, extending half-life to ~7 days. Promotes satiety, slows gastric emptying, stimulates insulin secretion. [Ref 1] |
| **Purported Benefits** | Significant weight loss; appetite suppression; glycemic control; cardiovascular risk reduction (SELECT trial, PMID: [37385526](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37385526/) — *correction: see Ref 2*); kidney protection (FLOW trial) |
| **Typical Dosing** | 0.25 mg SC weekly × 4 wk → 0.5 mg → 1.0 mg → 1.7 mg → 2.4 mg/wk (Wegovy). Ozempic: max 2.0 mg/wk. [FDA label] |
| **Efficacy Data** | STEP 1 (PMID: [33567185](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33567185/)): **−14.9%** body weight vs −2.4% placebo at 68 wk (n=1,961). 86.4% achieved ≥5% loss; 50.5% achieved ≥15%. [Ref 1] |
| **Side Effects** | Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation (GI most common, typically transient); GERD; pancreatitis (rare); thyroid C-cell tumors in rodents (boxed warning); gallbladder disease; injection-site reactions [Ref 1] |
| **Human Research** | ★★★★★ **Extensive / FDA-Approved** — STEP 1–5 trials; SUSTAIN 1–10 (T2D); SELECT (CV outcomes); FLOW (CKD). Ozempic approved Dec 2017; Wegovy approved June 2021. [Ref 3, 4] |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★ High — well-characterized risk profile from 25M+ US prescriptions annually |
| **Legal Status** | FDA-approved Rx; also sold as research chemical by gray-market vendors |
| **Price Range** | $1.05–$175.00/mg across 41 vendors ([PeptideCritic](https://peptidecritic.com/peptides/glp1-s)) |
| **Key References** | [1] Wilding et al., NEJM 2021;384:989-1002 (STEP 1) · [2] Lincoff et al., NEJM 2023;389:2221-2232 (SELECT) · [3] [FDA Ozempic label](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/label/2020/209637s003lbl.pdf) · [4] [FDA Wegovy label](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/label/2021/215256s000lbl.pdf) |

### Tirzepatide

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Also Known As** | GLP2-T · Mounjaro · Zepbound · LY3298176 |
| **Category** | GLP-1/GIP Dual Agonist ("Twincretin") |
| **Mechanism** | Unimolecular dual agonist at GLP-1 and GIP receptors. GIP agonism enhances insulin sensitivity and fat metabolism complementary to GLP-1. [Ref 1] |

| **Human Research** | ★★★★★ **Extensive / FDA-Approved** — SURMOUNT 1–4 (obesity); SURPASS 1–5 (T2D). Mounjaro approved May 2022; Zepbound approved Nov 2023. [Ref 2, 3] |

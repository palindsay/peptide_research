## How to Read This Guide

Every compound in this guide contains the following standardized fields:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | Alternative names, brand names, chemical designations |
| **Category** | Therapeutic classification |
| **Mechanism** | How the compound works at the molecular/receptor level |
| **Efficacy Data** | Quantitative results from clinical trials or preclinical studies |
| **Human Benefits** | Documented therapeutic effects in humans |
| **Typical Dosing** | Commonly reported research/clinical dosing protocols |
| **Side Effects** | Known adverse effects and their frequency |
| **Human Research** | Summary of clinical trial status and key publications |
| **Safety Rating** | 1–5 star rating based on depth of human evidence |
| **Legal Status** | Regulatory/prescription status in major jurisdictions |
| **Price Range** | Market price per mg from PeptideCritic vendor data (March 2026) |
| **Key References** | PubMed IDs and primary sources |
| **Social Media Signals** | Community sentiment from GLP-1 Forum and peptide communities |

### Safety Rating Key

| Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ★★★★★ | FDA/EMA-approved; extensive Phase III+ data; well-characterized safety profile |
| ★★★★☆ | Strong Phase II/III data; approved in some jurisdictions; favorable risk-benefit |
| ★★★☆☆ | Moderate human data (Phase I/II); some safety signals; use with caution |
| ★★☆☆☆ | Limited human data; primarily preclinical evidence; significant unknowns |
| ★☆☆☆☆ | Preclinical only or single-lab data; safety profile essentially unknown |

### 1.1 Semaglutide

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | GLP1-S, Ozempic (0.5–2 mg SC weekly), Wegovy (2.4 mg SC weekly), Rybelsus (oral 7–14 mg daily), GLP-1 analogue, NN9535 |
| **Category** | GLP-1 Receptor Agonist / Incretin Mimetic |
| **Mechanism** | Long-acting GLP-1 receptor agonist. Binds GLP-1R on pancreatic beta cells, hypothalamic appetite centers, and GI tract. Stimulates glucose-dependent insulin secretion, suppresses glucagon, slows gastric emptying, and reduces appetite via central satiety signaling. C18 fatty-acid acylation to albumin extends half-life to ~7 days. |
| **Efficacy Data** | STEP 1 (2021): −14.9% body weight vs −2.4% placebo at 68 wk (n=1,961; PMID 33567185). STEP 2 (T2DM): −9.6% at 68 wk. STEP 3 (intensive behavioral therapy): −16.0%. STEP 5 (104 wk): −15.2%. Oral semaglutide 50 mg (OASIS-1): −15.1% at 68 wk. SELECT cardiovascular outcomes trial: 20% reduction in MACE (PMID 37952131). |
| **Human Benefits** | Significant sustained weight loss (12–17% in trials); improved glycemic control (HbA1c reduction ~1.5%); reduced cardiovascular risk (MACE reduction); improved blood pressure and lipid profiles; reduced liver fat; potential benefits for NAFLD/NASH, heart failure, and kidney disease (under investigation). |
| **Typical Dosing** | Obesity (Wegovy): Start 0.25 mg SC weekly × 4 wk, escalate monthly: 0.5 mg → 1.0 mg → 1.7 mg → 2.4 mg maintenance. T2DM (Ozempic): 0.25–0.5 mg weekly, max 2 mg. Oral (Rybelsus): 3 mg daily × 30 d, then 7 mg, max 14 mg. Research peptide: typically 0.25–2.5 mg SC weekly with slow titration. |
| **Side Effects** | Very common (>10%): nausea (44%), diarrhea (30%), vomiting (24%), constipation (24%). Common: abdominal pain, headache, fatigue, dyspepsia. Uncommon: pancreatitis (0.2%), gallbladder events (1.6%), injection-site reactions. Rare: anaphylaxis. Boxed warning: thyroid C-cell tumors in rodents (not confirmed in humans). |
| **Human Research** | Phase III complete (STEP 1–8, SELECT, OASIS 1–2, PIONEER 1–10, SUSTAIN 1–10, FLOW). FDA-approved for T2DM (Dec 2017, Ozempic), obesity (June 2021, Wegovy), and cardiovascular risk reduction (March 2024). EMA-approved. Over 50 Phase III/IV trials completed or ongoing. Among the most extensively studied peptide therapeutics in history. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★★ |
| **Legal Status** | FDA-approved (Rx). Schedule: not controlled. Available by prescription in US, EU, UK, Canada, Australia, Japan, and 50+ countries. Research-grade available from peptide vendors (non-pharmaceutical grade). |
| **Price Range** | Pharmaceutical: ~$1,000–1,350/month (US list). Research peptide: $1.05–$175.00/mg (avg $17.02/mg); 41 vendors on PeptideCritic. |
| **Key References** | PMID 33567185 (STEP 1), PMID 33568663 (STEP 2), PMID 34170647 (STEP 3), PMID 35441470 (STEP 5), PMID 37952131 (SELECT), PMID 36356032 (OASIS-1) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥🔥🔥 Very High — Dominant topic on GLP-1 Forum (40,322 members). Trending topics include dosing protocols, vendor sourcing, stacking with tirzepatide/retatrutide. "Recommended supplier" thread has 2,000+ replies. Community reports strong appetite suppression onset within 1–2 weeks. Weight loss of 15–25% commonly reported at 6–12 months. Side effects (nausea) widely discussed; slow titration unanimously recommended. |

### 1.2 Tirzepatide

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | GLP2-T, Mounjaro (T2DM), Zepbound (obesity), LY3298176, twincretin, dual incretin agonist |
| **Category** | Dual GLP-1/GIP Receptor Agonist |
| **Mechanism** | Synthetic peptide that activates both GLP-1 and GIP receptors. GIP receptor agonism enhances fat metabolism and may amplify GLP-1 effects on appetite and glucose regulation. C20 fatty-diacid acylation extends half-life to ~5 days. Acts on hypothalamic appetite centers, pancreatic islets, adipose tissue, and GI tract. |
| **Efficacy Data** | SURMOUNT-1 (2022): −20.9% body weight (ITT) at 72 wk with 15 mg dose vs −3.1% placebo (n=2,539; PMID 35658024). Efficacy estimand: −22.5%. SURMOUNT-2 (T2DM): −14.7% at 72 wk (PMID 37385275). SURPASS trials: HbA1c reduction of −2.07% to −2.37%. SURMOUNT-MMO (cardiovascular outcomes): ongoing. |
| **Human Benefits** | Superior weight loss to semaglutide in indirect comparisons (~20% vs ~15%); robust glycemic control (HbA1c −2.0%+); improved cardiometabolic markers (triglycerides, blood pressure, waist circumference); reduction in liver fat (NASH studies ongoing); improved insulin sensitivity; potential sleep apnea improvement. |
| **Typical Dosing** | Start 2.5 mg SC weekly × 4 wk. Escalate: 5 mg × 4 wk → 7.5 mg × 4 wk → 10 mg × 4 wk → 12.5 mg → 15 mg maintenance. Research peptide: typically 2.5–15 mg SC weekly with similar titration. |
| **Side Effects** | Very common: nausea (24–33%), diarrhea (17–23%), vomiting (6–12%), constipation (6–11%). Common: abdominal pain, dyspepsia, injection-site reactions, decreased appetite, fatigue. Uncommon: pancreatitis, cholelithiasis. Rare: anaphylaxis, angioedema. Same thyroid C-cell tumor boxed warning as semaglutide. |
| **Human Research** | Phase III complete (SURPASS 1–5, SURMOUNT 1–4). FDA-approved for T2DM (May 2022, Mounjaro), obesity (Nov 2023, Zepbound). EMA-approved. SURMOUNT-MMO cardiovascular outcomes trial ongoing. Over 30 large-scale Phase III trials completed. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★★ |
| **Legal Status** | FDA-approved (Rx). Not a controlled substance. Available by prescription in US, EU, UK, and expanding globally. Research-grade available from peptide vendors. |
| **Price Range** | Pharmaceutical: ~$1,000–1,100/month (US list). Research peptide: market pricing similar to semaglutide; typically $3–$20/mg from research vendors. |
| **Key References** | PMID 35658024 (SURMOUNT-1), PMID 37385275 (SURMOUNT-2), PMID 34170635 (SURPASS-1), PMID 36652991 (SURPASS-5) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥🔥🔥 Very High — Second most discussed compound on GLP-1 Forum after semaglutide. Frequently discussed in vendor threads (ERP 376 posts, Homopeptide 786 posts). Community reports superior weight loss vs semaglutide with similar or less nausea. Commonly stacked with retatrutide discussion. "What dose of reta are you at?" thread (221 replies) demonstrates intense interest in titration. |

### 1.3 Retatrutide

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | GLP3-R, LY3437943, triple incretin, triple-G, GLP-1/GIP/GCGR triple agonist |
| **Category** | Triple Hormone Receptor Agonist (GLP-1 + GIP + Glucagon) |
| **Mechanism** | Activates three receptors simultaneously: GLP-1R (appetite suppression, insulin secretion), GIPR (enhanced fat metabolism, incretin amplification), and GCGR (glucagon receptor — increases energy expenditure, hepatic fat oxidation, thermogenesis). C20 fatty-diacid with AEEA spacer on Lys17 extends half-life. Aib substitutions at positions 2 and 20 improve stability. |
| **Efficacy Data** | Phase 2 (2023): −24.2% body weight at 48 wk with 12 mg dose vs −2.1% placebo (n=338; PMID 37366315). At 24 wk: −17.5%. Meta-analyses of RCTs report mean weight loss of 15–24% across dosing ranges over 48–72 wk. Phase 3 TRIUMPH trials underway. Preclinical data suggests anti-tumor effects in obesity-associated cancer models (PMID 40094000). |
| **Human Benefits** | Potentially the most potent weight-loss peptide in development; pronounced visceral fat reduction; improved glycemic control; increased energy expenditure via glucagon axis; potential for NASH treatment (liver fat reduction observed); body composition improvement (muscle sparing relative to total weight loss reported in substudy; PMID 40609566). |
| **Typical Dosing** | Phase 2 protocol: Start 0.5 mg SC weekly × 4 wk. Escalate: 1 mg → 2 mg → 4 mg → 8 mg → 12 mg over ~20 wk. Research community: typically 0.5–12 mg SC weekly, slow titration essential. Some researchers report doses of 4–8 mg as effective balance of efficacy/tolerability. |
| **Side Effects** | Very common: nausea (16–43% dose-dependent), diarrhea (16–32%), vomiting (8–20%), constipation (5–16%). Common: decreased appetite, dyspepsia, abdominal pain. Increased heart rate observed (2–4 bpm; typical of GLP-1 class). No significant hepatic or cardiovascular safety signals in Phase 2. Low discontinuation rates (~6%). |
| **Human Research** | Phase 2 completed (PMID 37366315). Phase 3 TRIUMPH program initiated by Eli Lilly: TRIUMPH-1 (obesity), TRIUMPH-2 (T2DM), TRIUMPH-3 (obesity + CVD). Multiple systematic reviews and meta-analyses published (2025). Not yet FDA-approved; investigational. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★☆ |
| **Legal Status** | Investigational (not approved in any jurisdiction). Available as research peptide from gray-market vendors. Phase 3 clinical trials ongoing at Eli Lilly. |
| **Price Range** | Research peptide: typically $5–$25/mg from research vendors. Limited vendor availability compared to semaglutide/tirzepatide. |
| **Key References** | PMID 37366315 (Phase 2 NEJM), PMID 35985340 (Discovery/Cell Metabolism), PMID 40609566 (Body composition substudy), PMID 40094000 (Anti-tumor preclinical) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥🔥🔥 Very High — Trending on GLP-1 Forum. "What dose of reta are you at?" (221 replies) is among the most active threads. "Jeep Reta" (82 replies) discusses specific vendor sourcing. Community reports exceptional weight loss (some claiming >25% in 6 months). Enthusiasm tempered by lack of Phase 3 completion. GI side effects widely discussed; slower titration than trials recommended by experienced users. |

### 1.4 Survodutide

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | BI 456906, GLP-1/GCGR dual agonist |
| **Category** | Dual GLP-1/Glucagon Receptor Agonist |
| **Mechanism** | Activates both GLP-1 and glucagon receptors. GLP-1R agonism suppresses appetite and promotes insulin secretion. GCGR agonism increases hepatic energy expenditure, promotes fat oxidation, and reduces liver fat. Differs from tirzepatide by targeting glucagon (not GIP) as the second axis. |
| **Efficacy Data** | Phase 2 (PMID 38587233): −18.7% body weight at 46 wk with 6 mg dose vs −2.8% placebo. NASH trials: significant liver fat reduction (>50% relative reduction in hepatic steatosis). Phase 3 trials ongoing (Boehringer Ingelheim). |

| **Typical Dosing** | Phase 2: Start 0.3 mg SC weekly, escalate to 0.9 → 1.8 → 2.7 → 3.6 → 4.5 → 6.0 mg over ~20 wk. Research: 0.3–6.0 mg SC weekly with gradual titration. |
| **Side Effects** | Nausea (33–49%), diarrhea (19–28%), vomiting (15–21%), decreased appetite, injection-site reactions. Somewhat higher GI rate than semaglutide, likely due to glucagon component. |
| **Human Research** | Phase 2 completed. Phase 3 trials underway (Boehringer Ingelheim SYNCHRONIZE program). Investigational for obesity and NASH. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★☆☆ |
| **Legal Status** | Investigational. Not approved in any jurisdiction. Available from select research peptide vendors. |
| **Price Range** | Research peptide: $8–$30/mg. Limited vendor availability. Listed on PeptideCritic. |
| **Key References** | PMID 38587233 (Phase 2 weight loss), NCT05941312 (SYNCHRONIZE Phase 3) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥 Moderate — Discussed less frequently than retatrutide but growing interest. Community views it as a potentially superior NASH treatment. Some early user reports emerging on GLP-1 Forum. |

### 2.1 Tesamorelin

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | TH9507, Egrifta/Egrifta SV, GHRH(1-44) analog |
| **Category** | Growth Hormone-Releasing Hormone (GHRH) Analog |
| **Mechanism** | Stabilized synthetic analog of GHRH(1-44) with trans-3-hexenoic acid acylation at Tyr1. Binds GHRH receptors on anterior pituitary somatotrophs, stimulating pulsatile growth hormone release. Increases endogenous GH/IGF-1 through physiological pulsatile pattern, preserving feedback mechanisms. |
| **Efficacy Data** | Phase III trials: −18% reduction in visceral adipose tissue (VAT) at 26 wk vs placebo (PMID 20668050). Trunk fat reduction: −7.4%. IGF-1 increase: +81 ng/mL. Maintained effects over 52 wk of continuous treatment. Cognitive benefit (COGHAT study in HIV): improved executive function and verbal memory (PMID 31082549). |
| **Human Benefits** | Reduced visceral (abdominal) fat; increased GH/IGF-1 levels; improved body composition without significant muscle mass changes; potential cognitive benefits (memory, executive function); reduced liver fat; improved lipid profiles (triglycerides). |
| **Typical Dosing** | FDA-approved: 2 mg SC daily (abdomen). Research protocols: 1–2 mg SC daily, typically at bedtime or morning on empty stomach. Some protocols use 5-days-on/2-days-off cycling. Standard vial: 10 mg. |
| **Side Effects** | Common: injection-site reactions (erythema, pruritus, pain; 8–25%), arthralgia (13%), peripheral edema (6%), myalgia (4%), carpal tunnel symptoms (2–4%). IGF-1 elevation may increase theoretical cancer risk with long-term use. Contraindicated in active malignancy. Fluid retention usually resolves within weeks. |
| **Human Research** | Phase III completed (multiple trials in HIV-associated lipodystrophy). FDA-approved (Nov 2010, Egrifta; 2019, Egrifta SV). Extensive post-marketing surveillance. COGHAT trial (cognitive outcomes). Ongoing research in NAFLD and aging. Among the best-studied GHRH analogs. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★★ |
| **Legal Status** | FDA-approved (Rx) for HIV-associated lipodystrophy. Off-label use for other indications. Not a controlled substance. Research-grade available from 67 vendors on PeptideCritic ($0.65–$52.00/mg). |
| **Price Range** | Pharmaceutical: ~$800–1,500/month. Research peptide: $0.65–$52.00/mg (avg $9.01/mg); 67 vendors on PeptideCritic. |
| **Key References** | PMID 20668050 (Phase III VAT), PMID 31082549 (COGHAT cognitive), PMID 24522462 (long-term safety) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥🔥 High — Widely discussed in peptide communities. "I'm 55 should I take Tesamorelin or just go for HGH and TRT?" is an active GLP-1 Forum thread. Community reports improved body composition, better sleep, reduced belly fat. Generally viewed as the gold-standard GHRH analog. Preferred over sermorelin by experienced users due to better VAT data. |

### 2.2 Sermorelin

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | GRF 1-29, GHRH(1-29), Geref, growth hormone releasing factor |
| **Category** | Growth Hormone-Releasing Hormone Fragment |
| **Mechanism** | Synthetic 29-amino acid peptide corresponding to the first 29 amino acids of GHRH — the minimum fragment retaining full biological activity at the GHRH receptor.

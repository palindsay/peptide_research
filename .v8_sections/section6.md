## 6. Mitochondrial & Longevity Peptides

### 6.1 SS-31 (Elamipretide, Bendavia)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | Elamipretide, Bendavia, MTP-131, FORZINITY™ (brand), D-Arg-Dmt-Lys-Phe-NH₂ |
| **Category** | Mitochondrial-targeted tetrapeptide; cardiolipin-binding agent |
| **Mechanism** | Aromatic-cationic tetrapeptide that concentrates in the inner mitochondrial membrane and binds electrostatically to cardiolipin. Stabilizes cristae curvature, preserves ETC supercomplex assembly, enhances cyt-c electron-carrier activity while suppressing its peroxidase activity → restored ATP and reduced ROS. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elamipretide)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | TAZPOWER Phase 2/3 crossover (NCT03098797) in Barth syndrome: >45% improvement in knee extensor muscle strength vs placebo, correlating with 6-min walk gains. ([PMID 32554501](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32554501/)) |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed:** Improved muscle strength and exercise tolerance in Barth syndrome (FDA-approved Sept 2025). Failed Phase 3 in primary mito myopathy (MMPOWER-3). Investigated in dry AMD and HFpEF. |
| **Layman Benefits** | Boosts mitochondrial energy output; improves muscle strength and exercise capacity in inherited mitochondrial disease. |
| **Layman Risks** | Injection-site reactions common. Long-term safety in healthy users unknown; anti-aging claims in healthy adults are extrapolated from mouse data only. |
| **Typical Dosing** | FORZINITY (Sept 2025 label): 40 mg SC once daily for patients ≥30 kg with Barth syndrome. Research: 40-60 mg SC daily. |
| **Side Effects** | Injection-site erythema/pain/pruritus (>50%), headache, nausea; rare hypersensitivity. No hepatic/renal signals in Phase 3. |
| **Human Research** | TAZPOWER (NCT03098797); MMPOWER-3 (NCT03323749, failed); ReCLAIM-2 (AMD); PROGRESS-HF. Orphan Drug, Fast Track, Priority Review, Rare Pediatric designations. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★☆ (well-characterized under supervised clinical use) |
| **Legal Status** | **FDA-approved (FORZINITY accelerated approval, 19 Sept 2025)** for Barth syndrome in patients ≥30 kg — Rx only. Original NDA 215244 received CRL May 2024; resubmitted with knee-extensor strength as intermediate endpoint. Orphan drug; WADA-prohibited (S2). |
| **Price Range (BiotechPeptides)** | Research-grade SS-31: ~$85-140 per 10 mg; ~$55-80 per 5 mg. FORZINITY pharmacy price undisclosed at launch (orphan-tier, est. $250k+/yr). |
| **Key References** | [PMID 32554501](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32554501/) · [PMID 32271091](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32271091/) · [PMID 39940746](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39940746/) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥 Major surge post Sept-2025 FDA approval; "first ever mitochondrial drug" narrative on longevity Twitter. |

### 6.2 MOTS-c

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | Mitochondrial ORF of 12S rRNA-c; MT-RNR1-derived peptide |
| **Category** | Mitochondrial-derived peptide (MDP); exerkine; metabolic regulator |
| **Mechanism** | 16-aa peptide (MRWQEMGYIFYPRKLR) encoded within MT-RNR1 (12S rRNA). Under metabolic stress/exercise, translocates from mitochondria to nucleus to regulate stress-response and metabolic genes via AMPK activation; enhances GLUT4-mediated glucose uptake in skeletal muscle. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MOTS-c)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | Lee et al., Cell Metab 2015: prevents diet-induced obesity and insulin resistance in mice. Nat Commun 2021: thrice-weekly MOTS-c from 23.5 mo extended physical capacity and healthspan in aged mice; exercise induces endogenous MOTS-c in human skeletal muscle and plasma. ([PMID 25738459](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25738459/)) |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed:** Endogenous plasma MOTS-c rises with acute exercise (confirmed exerkine). **No controlled human trials of exogenous MOTS-c for any indication as of April 2026.** |
| **Layman Benefits** | May mimic exercise benefits: better glucose handling, fat burning, endurance — in mice. |
| **Layman Risks** | Zero human safety data. WADA-banned 2024. Gray-market endotoxin/purity risk. Longevity claims unvalidated in humans. |
| **Typical Dosing** | Research (mouse): 0.5-5 mg/kg IP 3×/week. Underground human (NOT medically validated): 5-10 mg SC daily × 10-14 days, cycled. No established human ED50. |
| **Side Effects** | Injection site reactions; theoretical hypoglycemia from enhanced insulin sensitivity. No controlled human safety data. |
| **Human Research** | No interventional trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov. Observational biomarker studies in insulin resistance, NAFLD, VO₂max. CohBar discontinued its MOTS-c analog program in 2023. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★☆☆☆ (preclinical only) |
| **Legal Status** | Not FDA-approved; research-use-only grey market. WADA Prohibited List S2 since 2024. |
| **Price Range (BiotechPeptides)** | 10 mg ~$70-110; 5 mg ~$40-60. |
| **Key References** | [PMID 25738459](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25738459/) · [PMID 33547333](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33547333/) · [PMID 27216708](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27216708/) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥 Heavy biohacker hype as "exercise in a vial" 2024-2026; evidence lags marketing. |

### 6.3 NAD+ (and precursors NMN, NR)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide; precursors: NMN (β-nicotinamide mononucleotide), NR (nicotinamide riboside, Niagen®), NADH, nicotinamide |
| **Category** | Redox coenzyme / small molecule (not a peptide — included for longevity completeness); sirtuin and PARP substrate |
| **Mechanism** | Obligate cofactor for sirtuins (SIRT1-7), PARPs, CD38, and ETC oxidoreductases. Cellular NAD+ declines ~50% by age 60. Precursors restore NAD+ via salvage: NR → NMN → NAD+ intracellularly; extracellular NMN typically requires conversion to NR before membrane transport. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotinamide_adenine_dinucleotide)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | NR: dose-dependent 1.5-2.7× increase in whole-blood NAD+ at 250-1000 mg/day (Trammell 2016, Airhart 2017, Dollerup 2020). NMN (Yoshino, Igarashi): modest improvement in aerobic capacity in older adults and insulin sensitivity in pre-diabetic postmenopausal women. No human trial demonstrates lifespan extension. ([PMID 31127895](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31127895/)) |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed:** Raises blood NAD+; modest VO₂max, walking-distance, and insulin-sensitivity gains in older adults; symptom improvement in a 2024 long-COVID RCT. Cognitive/anti-aging claims unproven. |
| **Layman Benefits** | Restores age-related drop in cellular "fuel cofactor"; marketed for energy, cognition, healthy aging. |
| **Layman Risks** | IV NAD+ clinics are unregulated. High-dose NMN (>1 g) can cause flushing and nausea. Theoretical concern: sirtuin/PARP/CD38 activation may fuel some cancers — avoid in active malignancy. |
| **Typical Dosing** | NR: 300-1000 mg PO daily (studied up to 2 g/day). NMN: 250-1000 mg PO daily. NAD+ IV: 250-1000 mg over 2-4 hours (unvalidated). Oral NAD+ is poorly bioavailable. |
| **Side Effects** | Flushing, nausea, GI upset, headache. Generally well tolerated short-term. Long-term (>1 yr) human safety sparse. |
| **Human Research** | >50 human RCTs of NR; >15 of NMN. NCT04809935 (NMN aging); NCT04040959 (NR long-COVID). MIB-626 (Metro Biotech, Sinclair-cofounded) in Phase 2. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★☆ (oral NR/NMN short-term); ★★★☆☆ (IV NAD+ wellness clinics) |
| **Legal Status** | **NR:** FDA GRAS (GRN 635) since 2016 — legal supplement. **NMN:** FDA excluded from supplement status October 2022 via drug-preclusion clause; **FDA reversed this stance 29 Sept 2025** and confirmed lawful supplement status in Dec 2 2025 letters. **NMN is currently lawful as a dietary supplement in the U.S. as of April 2026.** **NAD+:** not FDA-approved; IV NAD+ infusions in regulatory gray zone. |
| **Price Range (BiotechPeptides)** | Not a peptide-vendor item. Retail: NR (Tru Niagen) ~$50/300 mg × 30; NMN bulk ~$40-80/30 g; IV NAD+ clinic infusion ~$400-900. |
| **Key References** | [PMID 31127895](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31127895/) · [PMID 27721479](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27721479/) · [PMID 33888596](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33888596/) · [FDA GRN 635 NR](https://www.fda.gov/food/gras-notice-inventory/agency-response-letter-gras-notice-no-grn-000635) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥 Sustained hype — Sinclair/Huberman/Attia platforms; 🆕 "NMN is back" wave following Sept/Dec 2025 FDA reversal. |

### 6.4 Humanin

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | HN; HNG (S14G-humanin, potent analog); Colivelin (hybrid analog); MT-RNR2 peptide |
| **Category** | Mitochondrial-derived peptide (MDP); cytoprotective exerkine; anti-apoptotic signaling peptide |
| **Mechanism** | 24-aa peptide (MAPRGFSCLLLLTSEIDLPVKRRA; 21-aa if translated in mitochondria) encoded in the 16S rRNA region of MT-RNR2. Binds GP130/IL-6ST + CNTFR + WSX-1 tripartite complex and FPRL1/FPR2 → activates STAT3, AKT, ERK1/2. Anti-apoptotic via sequestration of Bax and IGFBP3. Protects neurons from Aβ toxicity. Circulating levels decline with age; elevated in long-lived Ames dwarf mice. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humanin)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | Hashimoto et al. PNAS 2001: humanin rescues neurons from Aβ- and FAD-mutant-induced death; HNG analog 1000× more potent. Lee et al. Aging 2020: plasma humanin correlates with longevity; lifespan extension in C. elegans orthologs. ([PMID 11371647](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11371647/)) |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed:** Confirmed exerkine (rises with exercise and caloric restriction); biomarker of mitochondrial health — lower in Alzheimer's, T2DM, coronary endothelial dysfunction. **No approved therapeutic indication; no completed RCTs of exogenous humanin.** |
| **Layman Benefits** | May protect brain, heart, and insulin-producing cells from age-related stress — animals only. |
| **Layman Risks** | Zero human clinical safety data for exogenous administration. Gray-market vials unregulated. |
| **Typical Dosing** | No validated human dose. Preclinical: 1-10 mg/kg IP rodents. Underground human (unvalidated): ~5-10 mg SC daily. |
| **Side Effects** | Unknown in humans. Theoretical GP130-pathway immune modulation. |
| **Human Research** | No registered interventional trials as of April 2026. Extensive observational literature in Alzheimer's, CV, and metabolic cohorts. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★☆☆☆ (preclinical only) |
| **Legal Status** | Not FDA-approved. Not scheduled. Research-use-only grey market. Not explicitly named by WADA but covered under S2 peptide-hormone class when used for enhancement. |
| **Price Range (BiotechPeptides)** | 5 mg ~$55-90; 10 mg ~$95-160. HNG analog ~2× premium. |
| **Key References** | [PMID 11371647](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11371647/) · [PMID 23420980](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23420980/) · [PMID 33199292](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33199292/) |
| **Social Media Signals** | Moderate biohacker interest; typically paired in "mitochondrial stack" (SS-31 + MOTS-c + Humanin) — combination has no rigorous evidence. |

### 6.5 FOXO4-DRI (Proxofim)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | FOXO4-D-Retro-Inverso; Proxofim (vendor brand); FOXO4-p53 disrupting peptide; Baar peptide |
| **Category** | Senolytic; cell-penetrating peptide; protein-protein interaction disruptor |
| **Mechanism** | A D-amino-acid retro-inverso peptide derived from the FOXO4 interaction domain. In senescent cells, FOXO4 sequesters p53 in nuclear foci, suppressing apoptosis. FOXO4-DRI disrupts the FOXO4-p53 interaction → p53 releases → BAX/BAK activation → cytochrome-c release → caspase-3-mediated apoptosis selectively in senescent cells. Non-senescent cells spared. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FOXO4)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | Baar et al. Cell 2017: in naturally aged and XpdTTD/TTD progeroid mice, 5 mg/kg IV on days 1/3/5 restored fur density, renal function, and running capacity; neutralized doxorubicin chemotoxicity without thrombocytopenia (unlike ABT-263). Follow-up Aging 2020: selective senescent-Leydig-cell clearance restored testosterone. ([PMID 28340339](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28340339/)) |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed: NONE.** No registered or completed human trials of FOXO4-DRI as of April 2026. All benefits remain preclinical. |
| **Layman Benefits** | Aims to selectively kill "zombie" senescent cells that accumulate with age and drive inflammation — shown in mice only. |
| **Layman Risks** | **High-risk compound.** Off-target senolysis could harm quiescent stem cells, cardiomyocytes. "Proxofim" and similar vendors publish no third-party HPLC/MS audit data — endotoxin and sequence-fidelity risk is serious for a D-peptide dosed IV. Without physician oversight, no monitoring for thrombocytopenia, hepatic injury, or tumor promotion. |
| **Typical Dosing** | No validated human dose. Mouse protocol: 5 mg/kg IV × 3 doses on days 1/3/5. Underground human (NOT endorsed): ~1-5 mg SC/IV per dose × 3, cycled — extrapolated via BSA conversion, no human PK data. |
| **Side Effects** | Unknown in humans. Preclinical: mild transient weight loss at high doses. Theoretical: immune-cell depletion, impaired wound healing, cardiotoxicity, tumor promotion via selection of apoptosis-resistant cells. |
| **Human Research** | **Zero human trials.** No ClinicalTrials.gov entries. Cleara Biotech (de Keizer spinout) has not registered a Phase 1. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★☆☆☆☆ (experimental, unvalidated in humans, high biological risk) |
| **Legal Status** | Not FDA-approved. Not a scheduled drug. Sold as "research chemical / not for human consumption" by Proxofim and grey-market vendors. |
| **Price Range (BiotechPeptides)** | Not consistently stocked at BiotechPeptides. Grey market: 5 mg ~$180-350; 10 mg ~$300-600. Proxofim brand ~$200-400 per 10 mg. |
| **Key References** | [PMID 28340339](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28340339/) · [PMID 33067414](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33067414/) · [PMID 34041292](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34041292/) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥 Persistent hype on r/longevity as "the senolytic peptide"; 🆕 rising concern posts in 2025-2026 about vendor purity. Evidence-to-hype ratio exceptionally low. |

### 6.6 Epitalon (Epithalon)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | Epithalon, Epithalone, AEDG tetrapeptide, Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly; cross-referenced in Russian Bioregulators / Khavinson peptides section |
| **Category** | Russian bioregulator; synthetic pineal tetrapeptide; putative telomerase activator |
| **Mechanism** | Tetrapeptide Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly (MW 390.35 Da) synthesized from the AA composition of bovine pineal extract "Epithalamin." Proposed: (1) increases hTERT expression and telomerase activity in human fibroblasts; (2) binds methylated cytosines and linker histones H1.3/H1.6 → epigenetic modulation; (3) restores age-related pineal melatonin secretion. **Mechanism claims rely largely on a single Russian research group.** ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epitalon)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | Khavinson et al. 2003 (Bull Exp Biol Med): 33-45% increase in fibroblast telomerase activity at µM concentrations within 72h. 2004 fibroblast study: Epitalon-treated cells divided past Hayflick limit (passage 44 vs control 34). Russian longevity cohort data not independently replicated in the West. ([PMID 15582023](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15582023/)) |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed: VERY LIMITED / LOW QUALITY.** Only Russian-group data, mostly published in Bulletin of Experimental Biology and Medicine (low-impact). No independently replicated RCT demonstrates human lifespan extension or disease modification. Flag: evidence base is geographically narrow. |
| **Layman Benefits** | Marketed as lengthening telomeres, restoring melatonin, slowing aging. Evidence is weak. |
| **Layman Risks** | **Low-quality evidence base.** No Western-replicated human RCTs, no FDA safety review. Theoretical oncogenic risk — telomerase upregulation is a hallmark of ~85% of human cancers. Gray-market vial purity varies. |
| **Typical Dosing** | Common underground protocols: 5-10 mg SC daily × 10-20 days, cycled 1-2× per year. Oral forms have near-zero bioavailability. No validated human dose-finding. |
| **Side Effects** | Russian series report generally well tolerated — injection-site reactions, mild drowsiness. No long-term human toxicology. Chronic telomerase activation's oncogenic risk unquantified. |
| **Human Research** | Khavinson / St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation & Gerontology — multiple small open-label and observational studies 1990s-2010s; no registered ClinicalTrials.gov interventional trials. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★☆☆☆ (anecdotally tolerated, evidence quality low, long-term cancer risk unknown) |
| **Legal Status** | Not FDA-approved. Not scheduled. Different regulatory status in Russia (bioregulator class). U.S.: research-use-only grey market. WADA: covered under S2 peptide-hormone prohibition if used for performance enhancement. |
| **Price Range (BiotechPeptides)** | 10 mg ~$30-55 (low — simple tetrapeptide synthesis); 50 mg bulk ~$120-200. |
| **Key References** | [PMID 15582023](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15582023/) · [PMID 12937682](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12937682/) · [PMID 40141333](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40141333/) |
| **Social Media Signals** | Sustained niche popularity in longevity circles; 🆕 2025 IJMS review drove mainstream mini-wave. Critics flag single-lab evidence base. |

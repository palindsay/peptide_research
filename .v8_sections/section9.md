## 9. Reproductive & Hormonal Peptides

### 9.1 PT-141 (Bremelanotide / Vyleesi)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | Bremelanotide, Vyleesi, PT-141, AMP-3102 |
| **Category** | Melanocortin receptor agonist (reproductive / sexual dysfunction) |
| **Mechanism** | Cyclic heptapeptide analog of α-MSH; non-selective melanocortin agonist with highest affinity for MC4R and MC1R. Activates pro-erectile and pro-sexual pathways in the hypothalamus (paraventricular nucleus) independent of the vascular system, unlike PDE5 inhibitors. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bremelanotide)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | RECONNECT (Studies 301/302): pooled Phase 3 double-blind placebo-controlled trials in 1,247 premenopausal women with HSDD. Significant improvement in FSFI-desire (Δ+0.54 vs +0.27, p<0.001) and FSDS-DAO item 13. ([PMID 31135050](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31135050/)) |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed:** FDA-approved (June 21, 2019) for acquired, generalized HSDD in premenopausal women. On-demand subcutaneous dosing ≥45 min before anticipated sexual activity. |
| **Layman Benefits** | "Libido shot" — increases sexual desire and arousal via a brain-based mechanism, working where Viagra-type drugs fail. |
| **Layman Risks** | Nausea (40%), flushing, headache, transient BP spikes (~6 mm Hg systolic), focal hyperpigmentation with repeated use, injection-site reactions. Avoid in uncontrolled hypertension/CVD. |
| **Typical Dosing** | 1.75 mg SC (autoinjector) ≤1×/24 h, max 8 doses/month (FDA label). Research-grade: 1–2 mg SC on demand. |
| **Side Effects** | Nausea (40%), flushing (20%), injection-site reaction (13%), headache (11%), vomiting (4.8%), transient ↑BP 6/3 mm Hg with compensatory ↓HR; focal hyperpigmentation with >8 doses/month. |
| **Human Research** | ≥18 published trials including RECONNECT Phase 3 (N=1,247), HSDD Phase 2b (Clayton 2016), male ED studies (intranasal form discontinued due to BP). |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★☆ (FDA-approved; well-characterized but BP/nausea profile limits chronic use) |
| **Legal Status** | US: FDA-approved Rx (Vyleesi, Palatin/Cosette). Research chemical form sold widely "not for human use." |
| **Price Range (BiotechPeptides)** | 10 mg vial ≈ $55–75 |
| **Key References** | [PMID 31135050](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31135050/) · [PMID 27900364](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27900364/) · [FDA Vyleesi Label](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/label/2019/210557s000lbl.pdf) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥 High — dominant in r/Peptides and women's health forums; "PT-141 protocol" trending on TikTok 2025–2026 |

### 9.2 Gonadorelin (GnRH / LH-RH)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | GnRH, LHRH, Luteinizing Hormone-Releasing Hormone, Factrel, Lutrepulse, Cystorelin |
| **Category** | GnRH agonist (short-acting endogenous decapeptide) |
| **Mechanism** | Identical to native hypothalamic GnRH (pGlu-His-Trp-Ser-Tyr-Gly-Leu-Arg-Pro-Gly-NH₂). Pulsatile administration stimulates pituitary gonadotrophs to release LH and FSH via GnRH-R (Gq/11); continuous administration desensitizes the receptor and suppresses gonads. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gonadorelin)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | Pulsatile gonadorelin (5–20 µg SC q90min via pump) restores ovulation in >90% of women with hypothalamic amenorrhea and induces spermatogenesis/testosterone in men with congenital hypogonadotropic hypogonadism. ([PMID 3098273](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3098273/)) |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed:** FDA-approved (Lutrepulse 1989, Factrel) for primary hypothalamic amenorrhea and pituitary gonadotropic function testing. Off-label: TRT adjunct to preserve testicular volume, intratesticular testosterone, and fertility. |
| **Layman Benefits** | "Keeps the boys online" — men on TRT use it to prevent testicular atrophy and preserve fertility by mimicking the body's natural hormonal pulse (replacing HCG in many modern TRT clinics). |
| **Layman Risks** | Very short half-life (~2–10 min) means frequent injections; less testicular stimulation than HCG; headache, nausea, local reactions; downregulation if dosed too often. |
| **Typical Dosing** | TRT adjunct: 100–300 µg SC 2–3×/week or 100 µg EOD. Pulsatile ovulation induction: 5 µg IV q90min via pump. Diagnostic: 100 µg IV single dose. |
| **Side Effects** | Headache, nausea, flushing, injection-site reaction, rare anaphylactoid reactions, multiple gestation with pulsatile use (~5%). |
| **Human Research** | 40+ years of clinical use; pivotal trials by Crowley/Santoro in hypogonadotropic hypogonadism; extensive reproductive endocrinology literature. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★☆ (decades of FDA-approved use; excellent safety in pulsatile/intermittent regimens) |
| **Legal Status** | US: Rx (Factrel, Lutrepulse historically; currently compounded via 503A/503B pharmacies). Research form widely sold. |
| **Price Range (BiotechPeptides)** | 10 mg vial ≈ $45–65 |
| **Key References** | [PMID 3098273](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3098273/) · [PMID 8423247](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8423247/) · [PMID 29771807](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29771807/) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥 High — TRT clinics replacing HCG with gonadorelin post-2020 compounding crackdown; "gonadorelin vs HCG" trending |

### 9.3 Kisspeptin-10 (Metastin)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | KP-10, Metastin 45-54, KISS1 (112-121)-NH₂ |
| **Category** | KISS1R (GPR54) agonist — master regulator of HPG axis |
| **Mechanism** | C-terminal 10-aa fragment of kisspeptin (YNWNSFGLRF-NH₂); agonist at KISS1R/GPR54 on hypothalamic GnRH neurons, triggering GnRH release and downstream LH/FSH surge. Kisspeptin signaling is obligate for puberty onset — loss-of-function mutations cause hypogonadotropic hypogonadism. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kisspeptin)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | Seminara et al. established KISS1R mutations as cause of idiopathic hypogonadotropic hypogonadism ([PMID 14573733](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14573733/)). Dhillo group (Imperial College) showed kisspeptin-54 triggers oocyte maturation in IVF with lower OHSS risk vs hCG ([PMID 25058895](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25058895/)). |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed:** Investigational — IVF oocyte-maturation trigger (reduced OHSS vs hCG), diagnostic test for reproductive-axis function, treatment of hypothalamic amenorrhea, potential in HSDD (Phase 2 shows increased sexual/attraction brain activity). Not FDA-approved. |
| **Layman Benefits** | "Upstream libido and fertility switch" — turns on the body's natural reproductive hormones; promising for IVF without ovarian hyperstimulation, and early data show it boosts sexual/romantic brain responses in both sexes. |
| **Layman Risks** | Experimental — short-term human data only; long-term safety unknown; transient LH/FSH spikes, mild injection reactions. Not commercially available as a finished drug. |
| **Typical Dosing** | Research only: 0.3–1.0 nmol/kg IV bolus (acute LH test); SC 1–10 µg in fertility studies. No validated at-home protocol. |
| **Side Effects** | Minimal — mild headache, transient nausea, injection-site reactions; no serious AEs in Phase 1/2 to date. |
| **Human Research** | 30+ trials (NCT02187991 IVF trigger; NCT04748796 HSDD women; NCT03914378 male sexual function); Imperial College UK leads most work. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★☆☆ (clean short-term profile but investigational; no approved indication) |
| **Legal Status** | US/UK/EU: Not approved; research use only. Available from research peptide vendors "not for human consumption." |
| **Price Range (BiotechPeptides)** | 5 mg vial ≈ $65–90 |
| **Key References** | [PMID 14573733](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14573733/) · [PMID 25058895](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25058895/) · [PMID 15616016](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15616016/) · [PMID 36724004](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36724004/) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🆕 Emerging — biohacker and fertility buzz; "kisspeptin libido" searches up post-2024 Imperial HSDD data |

### 9.4 Oxytocin

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | Pitocin, Syntocinon, OT, "love hormone," "bonding hormone" |
| **Category** | Posterior-pituitary nonapeptide hormone (oxytocin receptor agonist) |
| **Mechanism** | 9-aa cyclic peptide (CYIQNCPLG-NH₂) made in hypothalamic PVN/SON, released from posterior pituitary. Agonist at OXTR (Gq-coupled) — triggers uterine smooth-muscle contraction, myoepithelial milk ejection, and CNS effects on social cognition, pair bonding, and trust. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxytocin)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | Pitocin: >60 years of RCT evidence for labor induction and postpartum hemorrhage control; prophylactic use in third-stage labor reduces PPH by ~50% ([PMID 23440829](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23440829/)). Intranasal OT social-cognition meta-analyses show small effects with high heterogeneity ([PMID 26439366](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26439366/)). |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed:** FDA-approved IV/IM for labor induction/augmentation, PPH control, milk letdown (nasal indication withdrawn). Off-label/investigational intranasal for autism social deficits, PTSD, anxiety — evidence inconsistent. |
| **Layman Benefits** | "Cuddle hormone" marketed for bonding, empathy, trust, and reduced social anxiety; obstetric gold standard for labor and preventing postpartum bleeding. |
| **Layman Risks** | Social/bonding benefits of intranasal OT are oversold — SOARS-B autism RCT (N=290) failed to replicate prior positive findings. IV use: uterine hyperstimulation, fetal distress, water intoxication/hyponatremia. |
| **Typical Dosing** | Labor induction: 0.5–2 mU/min IV titrated. PPH: 10 U IM/IV. Intranasal (research): 24 IU single dose common; chronic 24 IU BID–TID. |
| **Side Effects** | IV: uterine hyperstimulation, fetal bradycardia, hypotension, anti-diuretic/hyponatremia at high doses, arrhythmias. Intranasal: nasal irritation, headache, occasional mood changes; generally well tolerated. |
| **Human Research** | Thousands of obstetric trials; >100 intranasal OT social-cognition studies; SOARS-B (NCT01944046) largest autism RCT — negative. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★☆ (IV: FDA-approved with known risks; intranasal: safe but non-obstetric efficacy unproven) |
| **Legal Status** | US: Rx (Pitocin IV/IM). Compounded intranasal via 503A. Research peptide form widely available. |
| **Price Range (BiotechPeptides)** | 10 mg vial ≈ $50–70 (compounded nasal ~$60–120) |
| **Key References** | [PMID 23440829](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23440829/) · [PMID 26439366](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26439366/) · [PMID 21802544](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21802544/) · [FDA Pitocin Label](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/label/2014/018248s042lbl.pdf) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥 Steady — "love hormone" nasal spray popular in wellness/relationship spaces; scientific community increasingly skeptical of OTC claims |

### 9.5 HCG (Human Chorionic Gonadotropin)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | hCG, Pregnyl, Novarel, Ovidrel (choriogonadotropin alfa), Profasi |
| **Category** | Gonadotropin / LH-receptor agonist glycoprotein hormone (α/β heterodimer, 237 aa) |
| **Mechanism** | Placental glycoprotein binding LH/CG receptor (LHCGR) on Leydig cells (men) and theca/granulosa cells (women) to stimulate steroidogenesis (testosterone, estradiol, progesterone) and oocyte maturation/ovulation. Plasma t½ ~24–36 h vs LH ~20 min. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_chorionic_gonadotropin)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | Restores intratesticular testosterone during exogenous TRT; 500 IU EOD maintains spermatogenesis and testicular volume (Coviello 2005, [PMID 15713727](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15713727/)). IVF ovulation trigger: 5,000–10,000 IU IM matures ~95% of mature follicles. |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed:** FDA-approved for (1) female infertility/ovulation induction (with FSH), (2) male hypogonadotropic hypogonadism, (3) prepubertal cryptorchidism without anatomic obstruction. Off-label: TRT fertility/testicular preservation, post-cycle therapy (PCT) in AAS users. |
| **Layman Benefits** | "Keeps testicles working on TRT" — preserves fertility and testicle size; used by men restarting natural testosterone after steroid/TRT cycles; medically used for undescended testicles in boys and IVF trigger. |
| **Layman Risks** | **The 'HCG diet' (500 cal + HCG) is debunked** — no weight-loss benefit beyond caloric restriction; FDA warning issued ([PMID 1580272](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1580272/)). Real risks: ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome, gynecomastia, water retention, mood changes, rare thromboembolism. |
| **Typical Dosing** | TRT adjunct: 250–500 IU SC 2–3×/week. Hypogonadism: 1,000–2,000 IU IM 2–3×/week. Ovulation trigger: 5,000–10,000 IU IM once. Cryptorchidism: 4,000 IU 3×/week × 3 weeks (pediatric). |
| **Side Effects** | Gynecomastia, acne, water retention, mood swings, headache, injection-site reactions, OHSS (women), rare thromboembolism, small risk of precocious puberty in boys. |
| **Human Research** | >70 years of clinical use; hundreds of trials across reproductive endocrinology, pediatric urology, TRT literature. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★☆ (FDA-approved uses well supported; weight-loss claims fraudulent) |
| **Legal Status** | US: Rx only (Pregnyl, Novarel, Ovidrel). WADA-banned. FDA 2011 warning against OTC "homeopathic HCG" weight-loss products. |
| **Price Range (BiotechPeptides)** | Not typically sold by research peptide vendors (recombinant glycoprotein); compounded 503A ≈ $50–150 per 10,000 IU vial |
| **Key References** | [PMID 15713727](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15713727/) · [PMID 1580272](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1580272/) · [FDA HCG Warning](https://www.fda.gov/consumers/consumer-updates/hcg-diet-products-are-illegal) · [PMID 22258722](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22258722/) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥 Mixed — still popular in TRT/bodybuilding; supply disrupted after 2023 FDA compounding actions shifted users to gonadorelin/enclomiphene |

### 9.6 Triptorelin (Decapeptyl / Trelstar)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | Trelstar, Decapeptyl, Gonapeptyl, Diphereline, AY-25650 |
| **Category** | GnRH superagonist (synthetic decapeptide) |
| **Mechanism** | GnRH analog with D-Trp⁶ substitution giving ~100× greater potency and longer half-life than native GnRH. Initial LH/FSH "flare" followed by pituitary desensitization/downregulation within 2–4 weeks, yielding chemical castration. Paradoxically, a single microdose triggers a sustained LH/FSH pulse useful for HPG-axis restart. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triptorelin)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | FDA-approved (Trelstar) for advanced prostate cancer; achieves castrate T (<50 ng/dL) in >94% of men within 4 weeks of depot ([PMID 10785707](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10785707/)). Single 100 µg SC microdose produces robust LH/FSH response useful as pituitary-reserve test and PCT. |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed:** FDA-approved for palliative treatment of advanced prostate cancer (Trelstar depot 3.75/11.25/22.5 mg). EU-approved for endometriosis, uterine fibroids, central precocious puberty, chemo fertility preservation. Off-label: HPG-axis restart / PCT after AAS or TRT. |
| **Layman Benefits** | "Reset button" for the reproductive axis — high-dose depot treats prostate cancer and endometriosis; bodybuilders use a single small dose to jumpstart natural testosterone after steroid cycles. |
| **Layman Risks** | Initial testosterone "flare" can worsen prostate-cancer symptoms in first 1–2 weeks (co-administer antiandrogen). Chronic use: hot flashes, libido loss, bone-density decrease, mood changes, injection-site reactions. Microdose PCT is off-label with limited controlled data. |
| **Typical Dosing** | Prostate cancer: 3.75 mg IM monthly, 11.25 mg IM q3mo, or 22.5 mg IM q6mo (Trelstar depot). PCT microdose (off-label): single 100 µg SC (or 100 µg/day × 3–5 days); some protocols 500 µg × 1. |
| **Side Effects** | Hot flashes (~59%), injection-site reactions, decreased libido/ED, bone loss with long-term use, mood changes/depression, hyperglycemia, rare pituitary apoplexy, initial flare. |
| **Human Research** | >30 years of oncology trials; extensive endometriosis and central precocious puberty data; small PCT/HPG-restart studies in AAS users. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★☆ (FDA-approved for oncology; microdose PCT has reasonable safety signal but limited RCT data) |
| **Legal Status** | US: Rx (Trelstar). EU: Decapeptyl/Gonapeptyl Rx. Research peptide form widely sold "not for human use." |
| **Price Range (BiotechPeptides)** | 2 mg vial ≈ $45–70 (research grade); Trelstar depot ≈ $2,000–3,000 per 3-month dose |
| **Key References** | [PMID 10785707](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10785707/) · [PMID 18694475](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18694475/) · [PMID 25201469](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25201469/) · [FDA Trelstar Label](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/label/2010/020715s022lbl.pdf) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥 High in bodybuilding/PCT forums — "Trip micro dose PCT" widely discussed on Reddit and Meso; oncology use steady |

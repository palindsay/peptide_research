### 5.5 P-21 (P021)

### 5.7 DSIP (Delta Sleep-Inducing Peptide)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | Delta-Sleep Inducing Peptide, DSIP nonapeptide |
| **Category** | Endogenous sleep-modulating nonapeptide |
| **Mechanism** | Nine-residue peptide Trp-Ala-Gly-Gly-Asp-Ala-Ser-Gly-Glu (MW 850 Da), isolated 1974 by Schoenenberger-Monnier from rabbit cerebral venous blood during induced sleep. Potentiates GABA, blocks NMDA, interacts with opioid system (naloxone reverses sleep effect in median thalamus); no identified receptor; sleep-pressure modulator rather than sedative ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delta-sleep-inducing_peptide)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | Schneider-Helmert 1981 (n=6 IV infusion): immediate sleep pressure, increased TST, decreased sleep-onset latency, improved efficiency without daytime sedation; Schneider-Helmert 1992 chronic insomniacs: improved sleep ([PMID 1299794](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1299794/)); later EEG studies mixed; 2024 Pichia pastoris DSIP-CBBBP fusion efficacy in PCPA-insomnia mouse model |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed:** Modest sleep-onset improvement in disturbed-sleep populations; small early trials report benefit in opiate (97%) and alcohol (87%) withdrawal symptom relief |
| **Layman Benefits** | Fall asleep easier when stressed; not a knockout - works on sleep pressure, not sedation |
| **Layman Risks** | No identified receptor - mechanism uncertain; long-term safety undefined; efficacy inconsistent across trials |
| **Typical Dosing** | 100-500 mcg SC at bedtime (empirical); historical trials IV 25-50 nmol/kg |
| **Side Effects** | "Incredibly safe" per 2001 EJA editorial - no lethal dose in animal studies; reported: transient headache, nausea, mild vertigo |
| **Human Research** | 1980s-90s small trials in insomnia, narcolepsy, addiction withdrawal, chronic pain; no modern large RCT |
| **Safety Rating** | 4/5 stars (exceptional tolerability; efficacy variable) |
| **Legal Status** | Not FDA-approved; research-use only in US |
| **Price Range (BiotechPeptides)** | ~$35-55 per 5 mg |
| **Key References** | [PMID 1299794](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1299794/) - [PMID 6895513](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6895513/) - [EJA 2001 Pollard editorial](https://journals.lww.com/ejanaesthesiology/fulltext/2001/07000/delta_sleep_inducing_peptide.1.aspx) |

### 5.9 Dihexa ⚠️ DEPRECATED

**⚠️ DEPRECATED (2025):** The primary research publication underlying all Dihexa efficacy claims - Benoist CC, Kawas LH, Zhu M, et al., "The procognitive and synaptogenic effects of angiotensin IV-derived peptides are dependent on activation of the hepatocyte growth factor/c-met system," *J Pharmacol Exp Ther* 2014;351:390-402 ([PMID 25187433](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25187433/)) - was **fully retracted in April 2025** ([retraction notice PMID 40312093](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40312093/)) following a Washington State University investigation that confirmed "falsified and/or fabricated" figure data, with co-authors Kawas and Harding found solely responsible. A prior Expression of Concern was issued September 2021 ([PMID 34551987](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34551987/)). Four related Harding-lab papers also earned expressions of concern. Athira Pharma (spun out of this work) settled False Claims Act allegations for >$4 million in January 2025, and its Dihexa pro-drug fosgonimeton failed primary and secondary endpoints in the Phase 2/3 LIFT-AD Alzheimer's trial. **The HGF/c-Met mechanistic foundation is compromised; the safety basis for human use has no peer-reviewed support. DO NOT USE.**

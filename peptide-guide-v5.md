## How to Read This Guide

Every compound contains 13 standardized fields. All PubMed references link directly to the verified publication. All vendor data links to live source pages.

### Safety Rating Key

| Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ★★★★★ | FDA/EMA-approved; extensive Phase III+ data; well-characterized safety profile |
| ★★★★☆ | Strong Phase II/III data; approved in some jurisdictions; favorable risk-benefit |
| ★★★☆☆ | Moderate human data (Phase I/II); some safety signals; use with caution |
| ★★☆☆☆ | Limited human data; primarily preclinical evidence; significant unknowns |
| ★☆☆☆☆ | Preclinical only or single-lab data; safety profile essentially unknown |

### 1.1 Semaglutide

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | GLP1-S, Ozempic, Wegovy, Rybelsus, GLP-1 analogue, NN9535 |
| **Category** | GLP-1 Receptor Agonist / Incretin Mimetic |
| **Mechanism** | Long-acting GLP-1 receptor agonist. Binds GLP-1R on pancreatic beta cells, hypothalamic appetite centers, and GI tract. Stimulates glucose-dependent insulin secretion, suppresses glucagon, slows gastric emptying, and reduces appetite via central satiety signaling. C18 fatty-acid acylation to albumin extends half-life to ~7 days. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semaglutide)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | STEP 1 (2021): −14.9% body weight vs −2.4% placebo at 68 wk (n=1,961) ([PMID 33567185](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33567185/)). STEP 2 (T2DM): −9.6% at 68 wk ([PMID 33667417](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33667417/)). SELECT cardiovascular outcomes trial: 20% reduction in MACE ([PMID 37952131](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37952131/)). Oral semaglutide 50 mg (OASIS-1): −15.1% at 68 wk ([PMID 37385278](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37385278/)). |
| **Human Benefits** | Significant sustained weight loss (12–17%); improved glycemic control (HbA1c ~1.5% reduction); 20% MACE reduction; improved blood pressure and lipid profiles; reduced liver fat; NAFLD/NASH benefits under investigation. |
| **Typical Dosing** | Obesity (Wegovy): Start 0.25 mg SC weekly × 4 wk, escalate monthly: 0.5→1.0→1.7→2.4 mg. T2DM (Ozempic): 0.25–2 mg weekly. Oral (Rybelsus): 3→7→14 mg daily. Research peptide: 0.25–2.5 mg SC weekly with slow titration. ([FDA Label](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/label/2021/215256s000lbl.pdf)) |
| **Side Effects** | Very common (>10%): nausea (44%), diarrhea (30%), vomiting (24%), constipation (24%). Common: abdominal pain, headache, fatigue. Uncommon: pancreatitis (~0.2%), gallbladder events (~1.6%). Boxed warning: thyroid C-cell tumors in rodents. |
| **Human Research** | Phase III complete (STEP 1–8, SELECT, OASIS 1–2, PIONEER 1–10, SUSTAIN 1–10, FLOW). FDA-approved for T2DM (Dec 2017), obesity (June 2021), CV risk reduction (March 2024). 50+ Phase III/IV trials completed. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★★ |
| **Legal Status** | FDA-approved (Rx). Available in US, EU, UK, Canada, Australia, Japan, 50+ countries. Research-grade from peptide vendors. |
| **Price Range** | Pharmaceutical: ~$1,000–1,350/month (US list). Research peptide: $1.05–$175.00/mg (avg $17.02/mg); [41 vendors on PeptideCritic](https://peptidecritic.com/peptides/glp1-s). |
| **Key References** | [PMID 33567185](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33567185/) (STEP 1) · [PMID 33667417](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33667417/) (STEP 2) · [PMID 37952131](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37952131/) (SELECT) · [PMID 37385278](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37385278/) (OASIS-1) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥🔥🔥 Very High — Dominant topic on [GLP-1 Forum](https://glp1forum.com) (40,322 members). "Recommended supplier" thread: 2,000+ replies. Community reports 15–25% weight loss at 6–12 months. Slow titration unanimously recommended. |

### 1.6 Eloralintide 🆕

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | LY3841136, selective amylin receptor agonist |
| **Category** | Selective Amylin Receptor Agonist (AMY1R) |
| **Mechanism** | Novel once-weekly selective AMY1R agonist — distinct from cagrilintide, which is a dual amylin/calcitonin receptor agonist. Acts centrally to regulate appetite, slows gastric emptying, modulates metabolism. Operates entirely independent of GLP-1/GIP incretin pathways, enabling non-overlapping combination potential. |
| **Efficacy Data** | Phase 2 (Lancet, Nov 2025, n=263, 48 wk): 9 mg dose: −20.1% (efficacy estimand); 6→9 mg escalation: −19.9%; 92% of 6→9 mg group achieved ≥10% WL; waist circumference reduced up to 17.1 cm; weight loss had not plateaued at 48 wk ([PMID 41109426](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41109426/)). Dose range tested: 1, 3, 6, 9 mg weekly. |
| **Human Benefits** | Up to 20% weight loss via non-incretin pathway; dose-dependent efficacy; improved lipid profiles; improved cardiometabolic markers; additive potential with GLP-1/GIP agents. |
| **Typical Dosing** | Phase 2: 1→3→6→9 mg SC weekly escalation. Phase 3 protocol pending. |
| **Side Effects** | Nausea (mild-moderate, transient); fatigue (26.9% pooled vs 11.5% placebo, up to 45.8% in 6→9 mg group); reports of malaise and affective disorders (attributed to caloric deficit mechanism, not direct psychiatric effect). GI tolerability appears favorable vs GLP-1 RAs. No deaths in trial. |
| **Human Research** | Phase 1 and 2 completed. Phase 3 enrollment initiated end of 2025 (NCT07282600). Lilly also evaluating eloralintide + tirzepatide combination Phase 2. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★☆☆ |
| **Legal Status** | Investigational. Not approved. Phase 3 ongoing. Not yet on research market. |
| **Price Range** | Not yet widely available from research vendors. |
| **Key References** | [PMID 41109426](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41109426/) (Phase 2 Lancet 2025) · [ClinicalTrials NCT07282600](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07282600) |

### 1.7 Orforglipron ✅ FDA APPROVED

| **Side Effects** | GI profile consistent with GLP-1 class: nausea, diarrhea, vomiting (mild-to-moderate). Treatment discontinuation 5.3–10.3%. No hepatic safety signal. Boxed warning: thyroid C-cell tumors (class effect). |
| **Human Research** | Phase 3 complete (ATTAIN-1/2/MAINTAIN, ACHIEVE-1/2/5). **FDA approved April 1, 2026 (brand name: Foundayo)** for chronic weight management in adults. First NME approved under FDA National Priority Voucher program. T2DM indication submission anticipated 2026. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★☆ |
| **Legal Status** | **FDA-approved (Rx) — April 1, 2026. Brand: Foundayo.** Approved for obesity/overweight + comorbidity. |
| **Price Range** | ~$299/month (US list price at launch). Lower than injectable GLP-1 agents. |
| **Key References** | [PMID 40960239](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40960239/) (ATTAIN-1 NEJM 2025) · [FDA Approval Press Release](https://www.fda.gov/news-events/press-announcements/) |

### 1.8 Tesofensine

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | NS2330, triple monoamine reuptake inhibitor |
| **Category** | Triple Monoamine Reuptake Inhibitor (small molecule, not peptide) |
| **Mechanism** | Inhibits reuptake of norepinephrine, dopamine, and serotonin. Reduces appetite and increases thermogenesis. Originally developed for Alzheimer's; weight loss discovered as side effect. D1/D5 dopaminergic interactions carry cardiovascular and psychiatric risk. |
| **Efficacy Data** | TIPO-1 Phase 2: −10.6% body weight at 24 wk with 0.5 mg vs −2.2% placebo (n=203) ([PMID 18950853](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18950853/)). Higher doses (1.0 mg): −12.8% but more side effects. |
| **Human Benefits** | Significant appetite suppression; ~10–13% weight loss; potential mood enhancement via dopamine; increased energy expenditure. |
| **Typical Dosing** | Oral: 0.25–0.5 mg daily. Start 0.25 mg × 2 wk then 0.5 mg. |
| **Side Effects** | Dry mouth (28%), insomnia (18%), nausea (16%), constipation (14%), HR increase (+8–12 bpm). CVD and psychiatric monitoring required. D1/D5 dopaminergic interactions have historically halted similar drugs in Phase 3. |

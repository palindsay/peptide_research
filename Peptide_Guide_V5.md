Every compound contains 13 standardized fields. All PubMed references link directly to the verified publication. All vendor data links to live source pages.

### Safety Rating Key

| Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ★★★★★ | FDA/EMA-approved; extensive Phase III+ data; well-characterized safety profile |
| ★★★★☆ | Strong Phase II/III data; approved in some jurisdictions; favorable risk-benefit |
| ★★★☆☆ | Moderate human data (Phase I/II); some safety signals; use with caution |
| ★★☆☆☆ | Limited human data; primarily preclinical evidence; significant unknowns |
| ★☆☆☆☆ | Preclinical only or single-lab data; safety profile essentially unknown |

### 1.1 Semaglutide

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | GLP1-S, Ozempic, Wegovy, Rybelsus, GLP-1 analogue, NN9535 |
| **Category** | GLP-1 Receptor Agonist / Incretin Mimetic |
| **Mechanism** | Long-acting GLP-1 receptor agonist. Binds GLP-1R on pancreatic beta cells, hypothalamic appetite centers, and GI tract. Stimulates glucose-dependent insulin secretion, suppresses glucagon, slows gastric emptying, and reduces appetite via central satiety signaling. C18 fatty-acid acylation to albumin extends half-life to ~7 days. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semaglutide)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | STEP 1 (2021): −14.9% body weight vs −2.4% placebo at 68 wk (n=1,961) ([PMID 33567185](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33567185/)). STEP 2 (T2DM): −9.6% at 68 wk ([PMID 33667417](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33667417/)). SELECT cardiovascular outcomes trial: 20% reduction in MACE ([PMID 37952131](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37952131/)). Oral semaglutide 50 mg (OASIS-1): −15.1% at 68 wk ([PMID 37385278](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37385278/)). |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed:** Significant sustained weight loss 12–17% ([PMID 33567185](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33567185/)); improved glycemic control (HbA1c ~1.5% reduction); 20% MACE reduction (HR 0.80, n=17,604, SELECT trial) ([PMID 37952131](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37952131/)); improved blood pressure and lipid profiles; reduced liver fat; FLOW trial: 24% reduction in kidney disease progression in T2DM+CKD (FDA kidney indication Jan 2025); NAFLD/NASH benefits under investigation. Oral Wegovy pill (25 mg daily) approved Dec 2025 for weight management + CV risk. Higher-dose Wegovy HD (7.2 mg SC) approved March 2026. **Community-reported:** 15–25% weight loss at 6–12 months commonly reported; slow titration unanimously recommended; "food noise" reduction most cited subjective benefit; muscle mass preservation concern drives protein intake emphasis (1 g/lb bodyweight recommended on forums) ([GLP-1 Forum](https://glp1forum.com)). |
| **Typical Dosing** | **FDA Wegovy SC label:** 0.25 mg weekly × 4 wk → 0.5 mg × 4 wk → 1.0 mg × 4 wk → 1.7 mg × 4 wk → **2.4 mg maintenance** (or 1.7 mg if 2.4 not tolerated). Total escalation: 16 weeks. If dose not tolerated, delay escalation 4 additional weeks ([FDA Label](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/label/2025/215256s026lbl.pdf)). **FDA Ozempic (T2DM):** 0.25→0.5→1→2 mg weekly. **FDA Oral Rybelsus:** 3→7→14 mg daily (must take fasting with ≤4 oz water, wait 30 min before food). **FDA Oral Wegovy pill:** 1.5→4→9→25 mg daily (no food/water restrictions). **Community research peptide:** 0.25–2.5 mg SC weekly with slow 4-week step titration; anti-nausea strategies (ginger, smaller meals) widely discussed. |
| **Side Effects** | Very common (>10%): nausea (44%), diarrhea (30%), vomiting (24%), constipation (24%). Common: abdominal pain, headache, fatigue. Uncommon: pancreatitis (~0.2%), gallbladder events (~1.6%). Boxed warning: thyroid C-cell tumors in rodents. |
| **Human Research** | Phase III complete (STEP 1–8, SELECT, OASIS 1–2, PIONEER 1–10, SUSTAIN 1–10, FLOW). FDA-approved for T2DM (Dec 2017), obesity (June 2021), CV risk reduction (March 2024). 50+ Phase III/IV trials completed. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★★ |
| **Legal Status** | FDA-approved (Rx). Available in US, EU, UK, Canada, Australia, Japan, 50+ countries. Research-grade from peptide vendors. |
| **Price Range** | Pharmaceutical: ~$1,000–1,350/month (US list). Research peptide: $1.05–$175.00/mg (avg $17.02/mg); [41 vendors on PeptideCritic](https://peptidecritic.com/peptides/glp1-s). |
| **Key References** | [PMID 33567185](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33567185/) (STEP 1) · [PMID 33667417](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33667417/) (STEP 2) · [PMID 37952131](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37952131/) (SELECT) · [PMID 37385278](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37385278/) (OASIS-1) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥🔥🔥 Very High — Dominant topic on [GLP-1 Forum](https://glp1forum.com) (40,322 members). "Recommended supplier" thread: 2,000+ replies. Community reports 15–25% weight loss at 6–12 months. Slow titration unanimously recommended. |

### 1.2 Tirzepatide

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | GLP2-T, Mounjaro, Zepbound, LY3298176, twincretin |
| **Category** | Dual GLP-1/GIP Receptor Agonist |
| **Mechanism** | Activates both GLP-1 and GIP receptors. GIP agonism enhances fat metabolism and amplifies GLP-1 effects. C20 fatty-diacid acylation extends half-life to ~5 days. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tirzepatide)) |
| **Efficacy Data** | SURMOUNT-1 (2022): −20.9% (ITT) / −22.5% (efficacy estimand) at 72 wk with 15 mg (n=2,539) ([PMID 35658024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35658024/)). SURMOUNT-2 (T2DM): −14.7% at 72 wk ([PMID 37385275](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37385275/)). SURPASS trials: HbA1c −2.07% to −2.37%. REDEFINE 4 head-to-head vs CagriSema: tirzepatide 25.5% vs 23.0%. |
| **Human Benefits** | **Research-backed:** Superior weight loss to semaglutide (~21% vs ~15% indirect comparison); SURMOUNT-1 15 mg: 57% achieved ≥20% WL vs 3% placebo ([PMID 35658024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35658024/)); robust glycemic control (HbA1c −2.07% to −2.37% in SURPASS trials); improved cardiometabolic markers; liver fat reduction; SURMOUNT-OSA: significant reduction in obstructive sleep apnea severity (AHI reduction); heart failure preserved ejection fraction (SUMMIT trial): reduced risk of CV death or worsening HF events. **Community-reported:** Faster weight loss trajectory than semaglutide; GI side effects perceived as more manageable with slower titration; community reports superior appetite suppression; muscle mass preservation better than semaglutide per anecdotal reports ([GLP-1 Forum](https://glp1forum.com)). |
| **Typical Dosing** | **FDA Zepbound label:** 2.5 mg SC weekly × 4 wk (initiation only) → 5 mg × 4 wk → 7.5 mg × 4 wk → 10 mg × 4 wk → 12.5 mg × 4 wk → **15 mg maintenance** (max). Total escalation: ~20 weeks. Maintenance doses: 5, 10, or 15 mg based on response/tolerability. Do not increase by more than 2.5 mg every 4 weeks ([FDA Label](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/label/2025/217806s031lbl.pdf)). **FDA Mounjaro (T2DM):** Same escalation, doses 5–15 mg. **Community:** Some users stabilize at 10 mg with good results; not everyone needs 15 mg; injection site rotation (abdomen, thigh, upper arm) recommended; refrigerate pens. |
| **Side Effects** | Very common: nausea (24–33%), diarrhea (17–23%), vomiting (6–12%), constipation. Same thyroid C-cell tumor boxed warning. |
| **Human Research** | Phase III complete (SURPASS 1–5, SURMOUNT 1–4). FDA-approved: T2DM (May 2022, Mounjaro), obesity (Nov 2023, Zepbound). EMA-approved. |
| **Safety Rating** | ★★★★★ |
| **Legal Status** | FDA-approved (Rx). Expanding globally. |
| **Price Range** | Pharmaceutical: ~$1,000–1,100/month. Research peptide: $3–$20/mg from vendors. |
| **Key References** | [PMID 35658024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35658024/) (SURMOUNT-1) · [PMID 37385275](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37385275/) (SURMOUNT-2) |
| **Social Media Signals** | 🔥🔥🔥 Very High — Second most discussed on [GLP-1 Forum](https://glp1forum.com). ERP (376 posts), Homopeptide (786 posts). Community reports superior weight loss vs semaglutide. |

| **Human Research** | Phase 2 completed. Phase 3 TRIUMPH program (Eli Lilly): TRIUMPH-1 through TRIUMPH-7 ongoing. TRIUMPH-4 positive topline December 2025. NDA filing projected 2027. ([PMID 41090431](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41090431/)) |

### 1.6 Eloralintide 🆕

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Also Known As** | LY3841136, selective amylin receptor agonist |
| **Category** | Selective Amylin Receptor Agonist (AMY1R) |
| **Mechanism** | Novel once-weekly selective AMY1R agonist — distinct from cagrilintide, which is a dual amylin/calcitonin receptor agonist. Acts centrally to regulate appetite, slows gastric emptying, modulates metabolism. Operates entirely independent of GLP-1/GIP incretin pathways, enabling non-overlapping combination potential. |
| **Efficacy Data** | Phase 2 (Lancet, Nov 2025, n=263, 48 wk): 9 mg dose: −20.1% (efficacy estimand); 6→9 mg escalation: −19.9%; 92% of 6→9 mg group achieved ≥10% WL; waist circumference reduced up to 17.1 cm; weight loss had not plateaued at 48 wk ([PMID 41207310](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41207310/)). Dose range tested: 1, 3, 6, 9 mg weekly. |
| **Human Benefits** | Up to 20% weight loss via non-incretin pathway; dose-dependent efficacy; improved lipid profiles; improved cardiometabolic markers; additive potential with GLP-1/GIP agents. |
| **Typical Dosing** | Phase 2: 1→3→6→9 mg SC weekly escalation. Phase 3 protocol pending. |

## 10. Bioregulator Peptides (Short Peptides)

All Khavinson bioregulator peptides share similar limitations: evidence primarily from the Saint Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology (Prof. Vladimir Khavinson, 1970s–present); observational studies only; not double-blind RCTs; not replicated by independent Western labs. Use with appropriate skepticism. Safety rating ★☆☆☆☆ for all (insufficient independent evidence).

**[Research] Background:** Developed over 4 decades of Soviet/Russian research originally aimed at protecting soldiers, astronauts, and athletes from extreme physiological stress. Ultra-short peptides (2–4 aa) proposed to regulate gene expression in target tissues via epigenetic mechanisms. Two categories: Natural Bioregulators (Cytomaxes — bovine organ extracts) and Synthetic Bioregulators (Cytogens — synthesized sequences). Multi-year Russian clinical trials (6–12 year follow-up) reported no significant adverse effects, but lack independent Western replication ([SeekPeptides Khavinson Guide](https://www.seekpeptides.com/blog/articles/khavinson-peptides-complete-guide)).
